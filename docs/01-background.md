
# project background

## 811

In Northern California,
[USAN811](https://usanorth811.org)
provides the essential infrastructure to
support 811 "call before you dig" laws.
They accept calls and create notification tickets.

Calling in is free.
Members of the USA North 811 organization
subscribe to relevant geographic areas,
receive dig notices for their area,
and pay fees proportional to use so
that 811 operational expenses are recovered.

We wish for Menlo Park residents to see the markings that facilities operators see.

## roles

Members of the organization are typically facility owners,
who have invested in burying pipes and wires beneath ground.
We seek to protect such investment by issuing dig notices,
so a backhoe can avoid disturbing buried facilities.

An excavator plans to dig, makes white line markings, and calls in a new ticket.

A facility owner / operator responds to a "locate request"
by marking buried facilities with paint.
For example, O'Connor Water might use blue paint to indicate
they have no water pipe facilities within the white delimited area.

## white line markings

CGA is the Common Ground Alliance.

In CGA's
[Best Practices](https://bestpractices.commongroundalliance.com/Portals/1/Best%20Practices%20Guide%20Version%2021%20PDF%20Download.pdf),
section 4-8 describes how "Facilities are adequately marked":

> Markings may include one or any combination
> of the following: paint, chalk, flags, stakes, brushes, or offsets.

No mention is made of electronic markings.
The next chapter describes a more permissive approach to locating:

> 5-2: Delineate Area of Proposed Excavation
>
> Practice Statement: The excavator delineates the area of proposed
> excavation by one or [more] of the following methods:

   1. Electronic white lining ...
   2. On-site pre-marking, also known as white lining, with white
      paint, flags, stakes, whiskers ...
   3. Clear description of the proposed excavation site on the locate ticket.

## electronic white lining (EWL)

The CGA Best Practices glossary defines EWL as

> The process in which an excavator identifies
> where proposed excavation will occur by drawing a polygon shape on a GIS
> map; that shape is delivered electronically by the 811 center to its member facility
> operators.

## digital haves and have nots

All Menlo Park residents can freely access the city's
[Permit Portal](https://aca-prod.accela.com/menlopark/Default.aspx),
which describes current and scheduled construction work.

Many city residents are Underground Facility Operators,
due to the buried irrigation plumbing and illumination wiring they maintain.
Very few of them have online access to 811 locate tickets.
In particular, they cannot see the Electronic White Lining
markings that a larger facility owner, such as O'Connor Water,
can see.

The city's Public Works department operates many facilities
and has already subscribed to an 811 feed of proposed excavations.

We propose to offer textual and GIS graphical summaries of
such excavations, complementing the street address information
already supplied by the city via the Permit Portal.
In the interest of transparency, we would like for
**all city residents** to know what Excavators and
Operators already know.
